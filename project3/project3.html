Explain the importance of responsive design and its impact on the target audience.

Responsive design is an essential consideration for modern websites to display properly on modern devices and thus be properly usable to the target audience. A responsive website means that its size/layout changes automatically based on the size of the screen displaying it, which is important, considering the wide variation in screen sizes used to browse the internet.

A website designed for a 1920x1080 monitor will display poorly on a phone unless it automatically scales itself to be more easily usable on the phone. Otherwise, potential users could be lost if they find it too difficult to navigate on their preferred device.

Google Analytics provides a wealth of data for free to help assess the success of websites. Describe three different kinds of reports that will tell you what information your target audience is accessing.

According to the Google Analytics home page, there are three useful and free reports provided by Google Analytics: Acquisition Reports, Engagement Reports, and Monetization Reports.

Acquisition Reports show how users are discovering the website. It shows what sources (such as links on other websites) lead users to the current website. This is useful for marketing because it shows how much of the traffic is organic and how much is from paid sources.

Next, Engagement Reports show how users interact with the website. That includes showing which pages users are on the most and what content on the website drives monetary conversions (such as users following affiliate marketing links from your site).

Finally, Monetization Reports show how much revenue the website is generating and from what sources, including ads, affiliate links, and user purchase activity.
